# Getting IT Help — A Quick Guide for Staff

This guide is for everyone, not just tech people. It takes about a minute to
read and covers the two things you'll do most: **submitting a ticket** and
**checking its status**.

---

## Before you submit: try the Knowledge Base

Some things have a quick fix that's faster than waiting. The **Knowledge Base**
(linked from the helpdesk home page) has plain-language help for the most common
issues:

- My monitor won't turn on
- How to request software access
- Connecting to the office Wi-Fi
- Password reset and account lockout

If one of those solves it, you're done — no ticket needed.

---

## How to submit a ticket

1. Open the helpdesk and stay on the **Get IT support** page (this is the public
   view — you don't need to sign in).
2. Fill in the **Submit a ticket** form:
   - **What do you need help with?** — a short title, in plain words
     (for example, "My monitor won't turn on").
   - **Describe the problem** — what happened, when it started, and any error
     message. A photo of the screen is great if you can add one later.
   - **Category** — pick the closest fit:
     - *Hardware* — equipment (monitor, laptop, printer)
     - *Software* — a program or website
     - *Network* — Wi-Fi, internet, or phones
     - *Account access* — passwords, logins, permissions
   - **Priority** — leave it at *Medium* unless you truly can't work (*High*) or
     it's a request that can wait (*Low*).
   - **Your name** and **Your department**.
3. Click **Submit ticket**.

That's it. You'll see a confirmation with your **ticket number** — write it down
or keep the page. The IT Support team picks tickets up from the queue.

**Tip:** The more detail you give up front, the faster it gets solved — it saves
a round of back-and-forth questions.

---

## How to check your ticket's status

1. On the helpdesk home page, find the **Check a ticket** box.
2. Enter your **ticket number** and click **Check status**.
3. You'll see the ticket's current status:
   - **Open** — received, waiting to be picked up.
   - **In progress** — an agent is working on it.
   - **Resolved** — the fix has been made.
   - **Closed** — finished (or no action was needed).

The page also shows who it's assigned to and any notes the team has added.

---

## What if it's urgent?

If a problem is stopping you or your team from working — an outage, or you're
locked out while on shift — submit the ticket with **Priority: High** and say so
in the description. For anything involving a possible security issue (a
suspicious email you clicked, a lost work device), submit it right away and
mark it **High**.

---

## Need more help?

If you're not sure how to describe the problem, just tell us what you were
trying to do and what happened instead. You don't need to use technical terms —
that's our job.
