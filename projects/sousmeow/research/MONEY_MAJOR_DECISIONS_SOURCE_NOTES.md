# Money & Major Decisions — source notes

Internal research notes. Public sources informed principles only. All user-facing Cookbook copy, prompts, examples, and checklists are original SousMeow writing. No proprietary templates, commercial packs, or long source passages were reproduced.

## Shared principles

- Reduce overwhelm through structure: timelines, checklists, questions, scripts, and worksheets.
- Never promise financial outcomes, approvals, savings, or legal wins.
- Never invent figures, rates, laws, regulations, medical advice, coverage, credit decisions, or program eligibility.
- Mark missing information; ask the user to verify with official sources or professionals.
- Redact SSN, bank/card numbers, insurance IDs, medical record numbers, and passwords before external AI use.
- Disclaim financial, legal, medical, tax, and lending advice on every Money Cookbook.

## Cookbook 1 — Build a Budget and Debt Payoff Plan

### Sources reviewed

- CFPB Your Money, Your Goals financial empowerment toolkit: https://files.consumerfinance.gov/f/documents/cfpb_your-money-your-goals_financial-empowerment_toolkit.pdf
- CFPB debt worksheet: https://files.consumerfinance.gov/f/documents/cfpb_well-being_debt-worksheet.pdf

### Principles extracted

- List income, expenses, and debts before choosing a payoff approach.
- Compare repayment approaches; do not declare a single “correct” strategy for every household.
- Keep the plan reviewable on a weekly cadence.

### Excluded / time-sensitive

- Specific program dollar amounts, interest-rate tables, and crisis-benefit thresholds that change.

### Transformation

Money snapshot → expense breakdown → debt strategy comparison → monthly budget → action + weekly review.

## Cookbook 2 — Prepare a Bill Dispute

### Sources reviewed

- Broad consumer-protection patterns: organize dates, keep evidence, communicate clearly, escalate calmly. (Principles only; no statute text copied.)
- CFPB consumer complaint and billing-dispute education pages (process ideas only).

### Principles extracted

- Build a timeline before calling.
- Separate facts, unknowns, and desired resolution.
- Prepare both a call script and a written request.
- Escalation plans stay open-ended when rights are unknown.

### Safety notes

- Do not invent state or federal rights.
- Use placeholders for account numbers.

## Cookbook 3 — Audit a Medical Bill

### Sources reviewed

- CFPB medical bills and collections consumer guidance: https://www.consumerfinance.gov/about-us/blog/know-your-rights-and-protections-when-it-comes-to-medical-bills-and-collections/
- Hospital billing education themes from public nonprofit guidance: understand charges, ask for itemization, verify insurance processing, document contacts. (Principles only.)

### Principles extracted

- Translate the bill into plain language without diagnosing.
- Verify dates, codes, patient identifiers, and insurance processing with the provider.
- Prepare questions and an inquiry/appeal letter without claiming legal outcomes.
- Keep a case timeline of contacts.

### Excluded

- Specific No Surprises Act clause language, dollar thresholds, and changing credit-reporting rules. Users are told to verify current official guidance.

### Safety notes

- No medical advice.
- Heavy redaction of MRNs and insurance IDs.

## Cookbook 4 — Navigate a First Home Purchase

### Sources reviewed

- CFPB Your Home Loan Toolkit: https://files.consumerfinance.gov/f/documents/cfpb_your-home-loan-toolkit.pdf
- CFPB monthly payment / homebuying worksheets: https://files.consumerfinance.gov/f/documents/cfpb_buying-a-house_monthly-payment_worksheet.pdf
- HUD-approved housing counseling referral concept (encourage locating counselors; no copy).

### Principles extracted

- Map stages before shopping.
- Affordability worksheets are planning aids, not underwriting.
- Compare loan notes with user-supplied figures only; never invent rates.
- Prepare separate question lists for lenders and agents.
- Viewing and closing checklists reduce missed steps.

### Excluded

- Current rate quotes, lender credits, down-payment assistance program details, and eligibility rules that change by locality and year.

### Safety notes

- Never invent approvals or program qualification.
- Remind users to verify with lenders, agents, and HUD-approved counselors.
