# Cookbook from public knowledge: Build a Study Plan

## Category chosen

**Learning & Teaching** (`learning-teaching`)

### Why it fills a gap

Existing Cookbooks cover marketing launches, SaaS validation, portfolios, YouTube planning, and novel planning. No Cookbook lived in Learning & Teaching. Study planning is broad, beginner-friendly, and a natural proof that SousMeow can turn public educational methods into guided AI projects.

## Complexity Gate (Product Law 002)

| Check | Result |
| --- | --- |
| Audience fit | Pass: for people who struggle with AI and with "just reread notes" |
| Expertise demand | Pass: Pantry asks only known facts (subject, deadline, hours, materials, weak spots) |
| Judgment visibility | Pass: prompts carry coaching; users confirm criteria |
| Fact honesty | Pass: invent-nothing rules on schedule, grades, and materials |
| Surface simplicity | Pass: five steps, beginner voice |
| Failure test | Pass: does not teach "learning science" as a profession |

Failed checks: **0** (gate passed).

## Public sources used for inspiration (ideas only)

1. Cornell University Learning Strategies Center — note-taking and "how to study" strategy family (public educational guidance).
2. Widely taught university "five-day study plan" patterns from academic success centers.
3. Public learning-science concepts: spaced practice and retrieval practice (educational psychology, taught openly in learning centers).

No wording, page layouts, or copyrighted explanations were copied. Examples (Introductory Statistics midterm) are fictional SousMeow samples.

## How the source ideas were transformed

| Public concept | SousMeow Recipe |
| --- | --- |
| Clarify what "good notes / ready" means | Define what finished looks like |
| Organize material before reviewing | Map the subject into study chunks |
| Plan study sessions on a calendar | Place practice on real days |
| Cue column / active questions / self-testing | Build your self-test kit |
| Short pre-exam plan | Plan the final week |

Beginner support lives in each Recipe's `why_it_matters` (what we're doing, common mistakes, retry advice) and in confidence-check help text ("you're ready when…").

## Reusable authoring pattern discovered

1. **Pick an empty category** with a real life job, not a platform feature.
2. **Name 1–3 public method families** (university guides, official docs), then ban their wording.
3. **Map method beats → Recipes** where each Recipe is one decision the user can approve.
4. **Pantry = known facts only**; prompts invent nothing about the user's life.
5. **Teach inside `why_it_matters` + checks** when the schema has no separate "common mistakes" field.
6. **Sample project** stays concrete and fictional so Demo Mode works without APIs.
7. **Pass Product Law 002** on paper before writing prompts.

This is a repeatable conversion loop: public process → SousMeow decisions → invent-nothing Pantry → teaching checks → finished files.
